## Context

Write in the Musical-Theater Comedy style: Character-driven POV with clear want/need and big theatrical hooks. Comedy emerges from character flaw and status play. Rhyme with clarity first, diction must enunciate, set up jokes with precise cue words. Use I-want statements and self-reveals.

## Instructions

Structure as: Verse 1 (I-want statement with flaw), Pre-chorus (hint at blind spot), Chorus (comedic thesis), Verse 2 (obstacles escalate), Bridge (self-reveal or twist). Use piano/guitar led theater-style backing with occasional key lifts and backing vocals as Greek chorus.

## Style

Theatrical comedy-pop, 115 BPM, piano-led, character POV, clear diction, chorus as Greek-chorus quips. Set stakes plainly, introduce flaws early, and ensure muddy diction is avoided through simplified rhythms on key beats.
